Our trained Behavior Technicians use ABA to teach several skills which will help improve the child\u2019s challenges in the area of communication, social skills, stereotypical behaviours and academics. Often, it is in a one-on-one setting.[\/vc_column_text][\/vc_column][\/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_custom_heading text=”Transitioning to Mainstream School” font_container=”tag:h2|text_align:center” google_fonts=”font_family:Bubblegum%20Sans%3Aregular|font_style:400%20regular%3A400%3Anormal”][vc_zigzag][\/vc_column][\/vc_row][vc_row][vc_column][vc_column_text]<\/p>\n

Part of our goal at The Zeebah Place is to make every child who passes through our system, become an integral part of the society. This includes eventually getting transferred to a mainstream school.\u00a0 <\/span>We, however, do not take the transition recommendations to a mainstream school lightly. The child will be evaluated\u00a0 <\/span>individually to look out for several abilities and coping skills. <\/span><\/p>\n

During the assessment for transitioning to a mainstream school, the following areas are considered:<\/span><\/p>\n

    \n
  • Academic skills<\/li>\n
  • Communication skills<\/li>\n
  • Social skills<\/li>\n
  • Behavioural skills<\/li>\n
  • Independence<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    For each registered child at our centre, a prerequisite assessment by our Clinical Team determines his \/ her deficiencies. An individualised program is designed based on the evaluation report.<\/p>\n

    Overtime programs are introduced, mastered and generalised to make sure the child applies these skills at home and other social settings.<\/span><\/p>\n

    \u00b7\u00a0 \u00a0 \u00a0 \u00a0 <\/span><\/span><\/span>Summary of our focus areas:<\/span><\/p>\n

      \n
    • ABA Therapy<\/li>\n
    • Occupational Therapy<\/li>\n
    • One-on-one and group, social skills training<\/li>\n
    • IT and computer skills<\/li>\n
    • Preparation for future employment and independent living<\/li>\n
    • Educators, Teachers and professionals who work with the children with special needs.<\/li>\n
    • General public with the use of Advocacy campaigns.<\/li>\n
    • Evidence-based research to find solutions affecting children and their families.<\/li>\n
    • Academic \/ Educational Support<\/li>\n
    • Speech and Language Therapy<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
